Introduction to the Reader

Dear reader,

Yes, this is a portfolio. It's content is not so different from any other portfolio, yet it is special. Do you want to know why it is special?

It's special because I made it special. It is an own website, it has it's own design, it's own navigation, even it's own hidden features.

After having had a lot of fun creating my last portfolio, which was mainly a collection of single webpages, I decided to go a step further and make the portfolio more interactive. So I added some Javascript-magic to dynamically load single articles without reloading the entire page and I sugar-coated it with some neat animations (as alway, newer browser = better experience).

Another thing that makes this portfolio special is that I am genuinely proud of it. I love the freedom of creating a portfolio however I want, because it enables me to combine my portfolio-work with learning techniques I wanted to learn for quite a while. In this specific example, I wanted to extend my knowledge of Javascript, so I decided to build this interactive page rather than linking a set of static pages. And the result really makes me proud.

In a nutshell, I want to say the following:

  • I really appreciate that, after 4 years, I finally have the opportunity to create my portfolio the way I want.
  • I think that it generally increases student's motivation to create a nice portfolio if they have the possibility to make it special - to create something of their own. And, as the medium is part of the message, I strongly believe that allowing them to choose the medium is a very important part in this process - especially in first and second classes.
