Neighborhoods of New York

Greenwich Village

Greenwich Village is located in West Side Lower Manhattan. Being founded in 1712, it is one of New York's oldest villages. It's Inhabitants are mostly in the upper middle class. One special fact about Greenwich Village is that it has named Streets, while most other Streets in New York are Numbered. One of the most important Buildings in Greenwich Villages is the main campus of New York University

SoHo

SoHo stands for South of Houston, with Houston being Houston Street. Before 1968, it was known as industrial wasteland, but it became very popular when artists started to live there. Today, SoHo is still known for it's artistic and creative inhabitants.

Little Italy

Little Italy is an Italian Neighborhood that is best known for the Festival of San Gennaro, which lasts 11 days. San Gennaro is the patron of naples. During the last years, Little Italy has mostly been absorbed by Chinatown. Today, it is only one street with a dozen Italian restaurants.

Chinatown

New York's Chinatown is the biggest and oldest Chinese communities outside Asia. Before 1965, only a few Chinese families lived there, but with the Immigration and Nationality Act, it became extremely popular. In the last years, luxury condominiums in Chinatown have become very popular.
